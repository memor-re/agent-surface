# Tactics Taxonomy

Use this reference to label what the counterparty is doing and to choose the right counter-move.

## High Anchor

Signal:

- the first number or demand is aggressively high

What it usually means:

- the counterparty is trying to define the range before you do

Preferred counter:

- reset the range with your own benchmark or structure
- do not negotiate from their starting point

## Scarcity Or Time Pressure

Signal:

- "we need this today"
- "others are waiting"
- "this window is closing"

What it usually means:

- the counterparty wants speed and emotional concession

Preferred counter:

- acknowledge urgency without panicking
- state what must still be aligned before commitment

## Authority Barrier

Signal:

- "we never do that"
- "this is non-negotiable"

What it usually means:

- the counterparty is trying to shut down discussion before exploring a narrower version

Preferred counter:

- reopen with conditional questions
- narrow scope, timing, or terms instead of challenging authority head-on

## Bundle Upsell

Signal:

- extra services, scope, or rights are introduced as the "better" package

What it usually means:

- the counterparty is increasing total contract value or moving attention away from the original ask

Preferred counter:

- ask for line-item logic
- compare bundled value with the narrower structure

## Final Offer Pose

Signal:

- "this is the final offer"

What it usually means:

- the counterparty wants to test whether you still have room or urgency

Preferred counter:

- introduce one last conditional trade
- test whether the finality applies to price only or to the whole structure

## Vague Terms

Signal:

- broad language without duration, scope, approval, ownership, or limits

What it usually means:

- risk is being pushed downstream onto your side

Preferred counter:

- force definition
- separate what is included from what is optional

## Concession Fishing

Signal:

- repeated asks for "small" additions without reciprocal movement

What it usually means:

- the counterparty expects free value

Preferred counter:

- make every addition conditional on a return trade

## Hidden Scope Expansion

Signal:

- the request sounds unchanged, but the work, rights, or obligations have grown

What it usually means:

- the counterparty is reframing the same deal as bigger without fully pricing it

Preferred counter:

- restate the actual package in plain language
- re-anchor around the expanded scope
