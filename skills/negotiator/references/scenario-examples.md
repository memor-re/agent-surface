# Scenario Examples

Use these examples as generic analogs. They are active skill examples, not provenance copies from a prior domain.

## Vendor Scope Expansion

Situation:

- a vendor quotes a base package, then adds reporting, approval rounds, and extended support as if they were minor add-ons

Leverage read:

- the vendor wants higher total value
- the user may not need all of the added support

Recommended move:

- restate the actual package in plain language
- ask for line-item pricing
- counter with the narrower scope first

## Partner Rights Request

Situation:

- a partner wants broad usage or reuse rights with no duration or channel limits

Leverage read:

- the rights have value even if the core service fee looks reasonable

Recommended move:

- separate base scope from rights
- narrow duration, territory, or channel
- price extension options separately

## Compensation Or Role Negotiation

Situation:

- an employer or client proposes a title, scope, or compensation package that is directionally attractive but underspecified

Leverage read:

- the user may have leverage through role fit, timing, or competing options
- the other side may want certainty before improving economics

Recommended move:

- separate title, authority, support, compensation, and review cadence
- define opening anchor, target, and walk-away before replying

## Stuck Budget Conversation

Situation:

- the counterparty says the budget cannot move

Leverage read:

- the "fixed budget" may still allow movement on scope, timing, rights, or renewal structure

Recommended move:

- stop pushing only on price
- ask what can move elsewhere in the structure

## Advisory Use During A Deadlock

Situation:

- Stabilizer or Council is holding a tense exchange where the user needs a calmer next move, but the final decision already belongs to someone else

Leverage read:

- the problem is framing, not authority

Recommended move:

- return a short situation read, likely tactic, tradeable levers, and a draft counter
- explicitly hand the choice back to the named decision owner
