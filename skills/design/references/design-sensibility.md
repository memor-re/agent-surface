# Design Sensibility

Use this reference to calibrate taste and process. Do not imitate a living person's voice, persona, or exact visual style. Translate the principles into memor.re's own product identity.

## Primary Lenses

### Jony Ive / LoveFrom

Sources:

- https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/tech-and-ai/our-insights/the-creative-process-is-fabulously-unpredictable-a-great-idea-cannot-be-predicted
- https://openai.com/sam-and-jony/

Design use:

- Protect fragile ideas long enough to prototype them.
- Write the problem before styling the surface.
- Use sketches, models, mockups, and prototypes to make ideas concrete.
- Treat engineering, product, and design as one practical creative group.
- Prefer humane simplicity: clarify what the thing is for, then remove only what does not serve that purpose.

Related people worth bringing in:

- Marc Newson: useful for craft, materiality, and object-level discipline when a surface needs tactile restraint.
- Brian Chesky: useful when product UI, service design, and founder-led quality need to align.

### Nikita Bier

Sources:

- https://x.com/nikitabier
- https://x.com/nikitabier/status/1958799724934570401
- https://www.mediapost.com/publications/article/407115/x-hires-app-designer-nikita-bier-as-head-of-produc.html

Design use:

- Treat user attention as scarce.
- Make every tap, notification, timeline item, and next action earn its keep.
- Prefer fast feedback loops and visible product behavior over abstract explanation.
- For social, feed, graph, and agent surfaces, design the interaction loop before decorating the frame.

Related people not canonized here:

- Elon Musk and X are context for Bier's current role, not memor.re design sensibility sources.

### Danielle Morrill

Sources:

- https://medium.com/@DanielleMorrill/introducing-mattermark-the-deal-intelligence-company-a9ed7c8a9872
- https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/mattermark

Design use:

- Use customer development and evidence to find the product category.
- Build interfaces around useful data, not generic dashboards.
- Watch for plausible ideas that are easy to explain but weak in practice.
- Let adjacent customer requests inform expansion only when they reinforce the core workflow.

Related people worth bringing in:

- Paul Graham: useful as a challenge lens for whether an idea is merely plausible or genuinely category-shaping.

## memor.re Translation

For memor.re, these lenses resolve to:

- relationship intelligence over decorative relationship graphs
- selected-node work over equal-weight panels
- provenance at the point of action
- two design loops before production movement
- compact, operational UI that respects repeated use
- data visualizations that clarify decisions, not just decorate the screen
